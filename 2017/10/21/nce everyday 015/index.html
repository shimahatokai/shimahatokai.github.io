Lesson 18 Porpoises

单词

  • porpoise a sea animal that looks like a large fish with a pointed mouth. Porpoises are similar to dolphins but smaller.

    dolphin a sea animal (a mammal ) that looks like a large fish with a pointed mouth. Dolphins are very intelligent and often friendly towards humans. There are several types of dolphin.

    两者最明显的区别是：porpoise 鼻子（snout）较短较圆不突出，牙齿是平的，体型较小。dolphin 鼻子（beak）更长更尖较突出，牙齿是锥形的，体型较大。大多数海洋公园的海豚都是 porpoise，因为更容易训练，外形也更讨喜。香港海洋公园的海豚是 dolphin。

  • formation (L. 2) a particular arrangement or pattern. 阵列。

  • waterlogged (L. 6) adjective, BrE /ˈwɔːtəlɒɡd/, (of soil, a field, etc.) so full of water that it cannot hold any more and becomes flooded. 浸透的，淹没的。

  • scent (L. 8) noun, the smell that a person or an animal leaves behind and that other animals such as dogs can follow. 臭味。

  • ensue (L. 9) verb intransitive, (formal) happen after or as a result of another event. 随之而来。

  • calf (L. 11) noun [countable] a young cow, a young animal of some other type such as a young elephant or whale. 幼崽。

  • swoop (L. 15) verb intransitive, (of a bird or plane) fly quickly and suddenly downwards, especially in order to attack somebody/something. 俯冲。

  • butt (L. 17) verb, if an animal butts somebody/something, it hits them or it hard with its horns and head. 顶撞。

句子

  • … it is probably a mistake to credit dolphins with any motive of lifesaving. (LL. 3 – 4)

    credit A with B, believe that somebody/something has a particular good quality or feature. 认定。

  • … as in riding the bow waves of a ship. (L. 5)

    ride something, float or be supported on water or air. 破浪乘风。

  • … after the turtles, who peacefully submit to all sorts of indignities. (L. 11)

    老版为：

    after the turtles, the Ferdinands of marine life, who peacefully submit to all sorts of indignities.

    the Ferdinands, 代指性情温顺的动物，源自1938年迪斯尼动画片Ferdinand the Bull，讲述一只名叫 Ferdinand 的温良公牛的故事。他只爱整日纳凉赏花，而不喜欢 butting his head with the other bulls。

  • … the next porpoise comes along and hits him another crack. (L. 17)

    come along, arrive; appear. 到来。

    crack (on something) a sharp blow that can be heard. 猛击。

文章

课文选自 Window in the Sea，米国 Rinehart & Co. 1956年初版，副标题为The Absorbing Story of Sea Life in a Great Outdoor Aquarium（水族趣闻），讲述米国佛罗里达州 Marineland 水族馆的故事，通俗畅销读物。

作者

Ralph Nading Hill (19 September 1917 – 10 December 1987) ，生于米国东北 Vermont 州 Burlington, 米国作家，环保主义者。1939年毕业于 Dartmouth College。写过不少关于米国 Vermont 州和新英格兰北部风土人情的通俗读物。

背景

killer whale (orca）（逆戟鲸，虎鲸，杀人鲸）也属于海豚的一种（ the oceanic dolphin family）。杀人鲸其实并不杀人，但会捕食须鲸，海豹，海豚等。

鲨鱼也不主动攻击人类，就像老虎不吃人，海里各种鲜美的鱼类吃都吃不完。但是由于人类的过度捕捞，海洋里的鱼类濒临灭绝，鲨鱼食物链断裂，自身也朝不保夕。近来不少鲨鱼冲上海滩自杀的报道，大概是因为实在活不下去了。而且人还捕鲨鱼，因为中国人喜欢吃鱼翅。

鲨鱼，老虎吃人其实是人类对自然界物种的妖魔化，这样人类就可以站在道德制高点上名正言顺地抓捕这些邪恶的动物。这就像孙悟空喜欢说别人是妖怪，然后一棍打死。其实他自己也是妖怪，不过是改过自新的妖怪。妖怪只要不害人，也是好妖怪，不要忘了唐僧说过 ‘妖怪也是妈生的’，只有孙悟空是石头里蹦出来的。

所以，不要去什么海洋公园，野生动物园参观。那些动物本来人家在大自然里生活地好好的，可是有人借科普的名义抓来关在笼子里供人观赏以谋利。每去一次，都是在变相鼓励去抓捕这些动物。真的要增长见识，可以去大自然里看他们。实在不行，也可以看看 BBC 的纪录片嘛。
