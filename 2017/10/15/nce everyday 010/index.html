Lesson 12 Banks and their customers

单词

  • current account (L.1) (British English)(US English checking account, Canadian English chequing account), a type of bank account that you can take money out of at any time, and that provides you with a chequebook and cash card. 活期账户。

    cash card (British English)(US English ATM card), a plastic card used to get money from a cash machine (= a machine in or outside a bank). 借记卡。

    cash machine (British English also cash dispenser, cashpoint™)(also ATM = automated teller machine)(also ABM = automated banking machine), a machine in or outside a bank, etc., from which you can get money from your bank account using a special plastic card. 取款机。

  • repayment (L.1) uncountable noun, the act of paying back money that you have borrowed from a bank, etc. 还款。

  • draw (L.2) verb, take money or payments from a bank account or post office. 取款。

  • primarily (L.2) adverb, BrE /praɪˈmerəli/, mainly. 主要地。

  • obligation (L. 5) countable noun, something which you must do because you have promised, because of a law, etc. 责任，义务。

  • complication (L. 6) a thing that makes a situation more complicated or difficult. 困扰。

  • specimen (L. 6) a small amount of something that shows what the rest of it is like. 样式。

  • load (L. 7) verb, put something into a weapon, camera or other piece of equipment so that it can be used. loaded against，比喻用法，‘用于防范 …’。

    load the dice (against somebody) [usually passive] put somebody at a disadvantage. ‘被算计 …’。

    laden adjective, heavily loaded with something. ‘满是 …的’。

  • customer (L. 11) a person or an organization that buys something from a shop/store or business. 顾客。

    client a person who uses the services or advice of a professional person or organization. 客户。

  • forgery (L. 12) countable noun, something, for example a document, piece of paper money, etc., that has been copied in order to cheat people. 赝品。

句子

  • banker-customer relationship is that of debtor and creditor … (L. 3)

    that of = relationship of.

  • … account is in credit or is overdrawn. (L. 4)

    in credit, there is money in the account. 账户余额。

  • Many of these obligations can give rise to problems and complications … (LL. 5 – 6)

    give rise to something, (formal) cause something to happen or exist. 导致。

  • … he instructs the bank to debit his account only in respect of cheques drawn by himself. (LL. 9 – 10)

    in respect of, (formal or business) in payment for something. 支付。

  • the bank has no right or authority to pay out a customer’s money … (LL. 10 – 11)

    authority (to do something) uncountable noun, the power or right to do something. 权力。

    pay out, pay a large sum of money for something. 破费。

  • … there is no risk to the customer in the practice. (L. 13)

    老版为：

    … there is no risk to the customer in the modern practice.

    practice, a way of doing something that is the usual or expected way in a particular organization or situation. 做法。

文章

课文选自 The Consumer, Society and the Law，英国 Penguin Books 1964年初版。此书多次再版。

作者

Gordon Borrie （老版新版都误印为 Barrie），全名 Gordon Johnson Borrie, Baron Borrie (13 March 1931 – 30 September 2016)，英国律师，御用大状（QC, Queen’s Counsel），伯明翰大学（Birmingham University）法律系教授及系主任。他16岁进 Manchester University 学法律，后成为消费者法专家（a leading expert and author on consumer and competition law），主张公平竞争。

Aubrey L. Diamond (December 1923–6 July 2006) ，英国律师，专注消费者法。伦敦经济学院（London School of Economics，LSE）法学本硕（1956），后在 LSE 教法律，1966年成为 Queen Mary College 法学教授。他曾是英国消费者联合会主席（National Federation of Consumer Groups），专攻消费者法。

背景

Gordon Borrie，Aubrey L. Diamond都是专业人士，又专精消费者法，本课行文之中可见多考虑消费者利益。
