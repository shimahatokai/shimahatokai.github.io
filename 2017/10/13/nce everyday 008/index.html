新概念每日一课 008

Lesson 9 Royal espionage

单词

  • minstrel (L. 1) a musician or singer in the Middle Ages. 中世纪的流浪歌手，街头艺人。

  • ballad (L. 3) a song or poem that tells a story. 民谣。

  • conjuring (L. 4) noun, [uncountable] entertainment in the form of magic tricks, especially ones which seem to make things appear or disappear. 变戏法。

  • penetrate (L. 5) adverb, succeed in entering or joining an organization, a group, etc. especially when this is difficult to do. 渗入。

  • thither (L. 7) adverb, (old use) to or towards that place. 往焉。

  • slack (L. 7) adjective, (disapproving) not putting enough care, attention or energy into something and so not doing it well enough. 漫不经心的。

  • horde (L. 12) noun, (sometimes disapproving) a large crowd of people. 人群。

  • commissariat (L. 13) a department of the army that is responsible for food supplies. 后勤部。

  • harry (L. 14) verb, (formal) make repeated attacks on an enemy. 袭扰。

  • patrol (L. 15) noun, [countable] a group of soldiers, vehicles, etc. that patrol an area.

    verb, go around an area or a building at regular times to check that it is safe and that there is no trouble. 巡防。

  • halt (L. 15) verb, stop; make somebody/something stop. 阻止，遏制。

  • assail (L. 15) verb, (formal) attack somebody/something violently, either physically or with words. 猛攻。

  • skirmish (L. 16) noun, a short fight between small groups of soldiers, etc., especially one that is not planned. 交火。

句子

  • Alfred the Great acted as his own spy … (L. 1)

    act as, perform a particular role or function. 扮演。

  • … visiting Danish camps disguised as a minstrel. (L. 1)

    此句完整应为：

    who disguised as a minstrel when he was visiting Danish Camps.

    disguise as, change your appearance so that people cannot recognize you. 易容。

  • These had settled down for the winter … (L. 6)

    settle down, start to have a quieter way of life, living in one place. 安顿。

  • …on the proceeds of raids on neighbouring regions. (LL. 8 – 9)

    proceeds (of/from something), the money that you receive when you sell something or organize a performance, etc.; profits. 所得，收益。

    raid (on something), a short surprise attack on an enemy by soldiers, ships or aircraft. 突袭。

  • There they collected women as well as food and drink … (L. 9)

    collect, go somewhere in order to take somebody/something away. 掳走。

    as well (as somebody/something), in addition to somebody/something; too. 顺带 …，还有 …。

文章

课文选自 Spies in Britain，英国Robert Hale Ltd 1964年第一版，190页。

作者

Bernard Newman，全名 Bernard Charles Newman (8 May 1897 – 19 February 1968) ，英国作家，间谍权威，写过大量关于间谍的小说和专著。一次世界大战时在巴黎做过卧底，二战时在英国文宣部（Ministry of Information）工作。

背景

Bernard Newman 是专业人士，读他的书比比看007靠谱多了。

Alfred the Great （849 – 26 October 899）， 英格兰 Wessex 王（871 – 899 在位）。课文中提到的 espionage 发生在 876 – 878年间，丹麦攻打英国，一开始 Alfred 惨败，后反败为胜。但893年，丹麦再犯英国，897年战败后再未踏足英伦三岛。
