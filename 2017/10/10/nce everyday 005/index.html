新概念每日一课 005

Lesson 5 Youth

单词

  • leave (L. 1) noun, (formal) official permission to do something. 许可。

  • fundamental (L. 2) noun, [usually plural] a basic rule or principle; an essential part. 原则，基础。

  • glorious (L. 4) adjective, (formal) deserving or bringing great fame and success. glorious future，锦绣前程。

  • splendid (L. 4) adjective, (especially British English) (old-fashioned) excellent; very good. splendid future，辉煌当年。

  • rub (L. 5) noun, the rub (formal or humorous) a problem or difficulty. 问题所在。

  • identity (L. 8) noun, the characteristics, feelings or beliefs that distinguish people from others. 个性。

  • dreary (L. 10) adjective, that makes you feel sad; dull and not interesting. 单调无趣的。

  • commitment (L. 10) noun, commitment (to somebody/something) the willingness to work hard and give your energy and time to a job or an activity. 投身。

  • violent (L. 13) adjective, very strong and sudden. 截然不同的。

  • conceited (L. 14) adjective, (disapproving) having too much pride in yourself and what you do. 自大的。

  • presumptuous (L. 14) adjective, [not usually before noun] too confident, in a way that shows a lack of respect for other people. 自负的。

  • fatuous (L. 14) adjective, (formal) stupid. 愚钝的。

  • elders (L. 15) noun, people of greater age, experience and authority. 长者。

    elder adjective, the elder who is the older of two people. 年长的。

  • reason (L. 15) 原因。

    cause noun, [uncountable] cause (for something) a reason for having particular feelings or behaving in a particular way. 事出有因。

句子

  • People are always talking about ‘the problem of youth’. (L. 1)

    are always talking，现在进行时加 always 表示频繁重复出现又有点令人疑惑的行为。

  • If there is one … (L. 1)

    one = a problem of youth.

  • … which I take a leave to doubt … (L. 1)

    take a leave to do something = beg leave to do something, (formal) to ask somebody for permission to do something. 恳求。

  • Let us get down to … (L. 2)

    get down to something/to doing something, begin to do something; give serious attention to something. 着手。

  • For one thing … (L. 7)

    used to introduce one of two or more reasons for doing something. 其一 …。

  • I find young people exciting. (L. 10)

    find verb, have a particular feeling or opinion about something. 觉得 … 。

  • They have an air of freedom … (L. 10)

    air noun, [singular] the particular feeling or impression that is given by somebody/something; the way somebody does something. 神情。

  • … they have no devotion to … (L. 11)

    have no = (BrE) have not got.

  • … in violent and lovely contrast with us suburben creatures. (L. 13)

    contrast, noun, a difference between two or more people or things that you can see clearly when they are compared or put close together; the fact of comparing two or more things in order to show the differences between them. 对比以见不同。

    suburben creatures = us. suburben creatures 为 us 的同位语（apposition）。apposition, the use of a noun phrase immediately after another noun phrase that refers to the same person or thing. 同位语紧接另一名词后。

  • I accept … (L. 15)

    accept verb, believe that something is true. 相信。

文章

课文选自 The Listener 1964年11月12日刊，The Listener 见 Lesson 2 介绍。

作者

Fielden Hughes（1899 – 1989），原名 Gwilym Fielden Hughes，作家，播音员，做过英国汉普郡 Alton senior boys’ school，温布尔顿（Wimbledon）Queen’s Road senior boys’ school 的校长，曾在 Eggar’s Grammar School, King’s College School 等学校任教。他的儿子 David John Hughes（27 July 1930 – 11 April 2005）也是英国知名小说家。

背景

Fielden Hughes 望子成龙，从小就希望儿子进牛津并成为作家，最终如愿以偿。David John Hughes 在英国皇家空军（R.A.F）服役（1949 – 1951）后，进牛津大学基督学院 Christ Church 读英语专业，最著名的小说是 The Pork Butcher (1984) 。

本课可以说是 Fielden Hughes 多年教书育人的经验之谈，为人父母的实践总结。
