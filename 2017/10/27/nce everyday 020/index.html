Lesson 23 Bird flight

单词

  • quite (L. 1) adverb, to the greatest possible degree, completely. 完全。

  • progress (L. 5) noun, movement forwards or towards a place. 前行。

  • purpose (L. 6) noun, [countable] the intention, aim or function of something; the thing that something is supposed to achieve. 目标。

    harness the air to their purpose，御风而行。

  • school (L. 7) noun, [countable] a large number of fish or other sea animals, swimming together. 群。

  • bore (L. 10) intransitive verb, to make one’s way steadily especially against resistance. 穿行。

  • passage (L. 15) noun uncountable, (formal) the action of going across, through or past something. 飞越。

句子

  • Duck, and like them the pigeons, are endowed with steel-like muscles … (L. 8)

    be endowed with something, (formal) naturally have a particular feature, quality, etc. 天赋。

  • … and these will ply the short wings with such irresistible power that they can bore for long distances through an opposing gale before exhaustion follows. (LL. 9 – 10)

    ply somebody with something, keep giving somebody large amounts of something, especially food and/or drink. 源源不断提供。

    gale noun, an extremely strong wind. 飓风。

  • The swallow shares the virtues of both schools in highest measure. (LL. 12 – 13)

    in no small measure, in some, equal, etc. measure,
    (formal) to a large extent or degree; to some, etc. extent or degree. ‘… 程度’。

  • It tires not, nor does it boast of its power … (L. 13)

    boast verb, talk with too much pride about something that you have or can do. 夸耀。

    boast something, have something that is impressive and that you can be proud of. 引以为豪。

  • … feeding its flown young as it flies … (L. 14)

    flown, adjective, 羽翼初长的。

    flown, verb, past participle of fly.

    the bird has flown, the wanted person has escaped. 煮熟的鸭子又飞了。

文章

课文选自 《田园之乐》A Countryman’s Creed，伦敦 Michael Joseph Ltd. 1946年初版，216页。此书记述作者追寻失落的英国农村田园生活。

作者

Sir William Beach Thomas, KBE (22 May 1868 – 12 May 1957)，英国作家，记者，以战事报道和田园生活散文出名。1891年牛津大学Christ Church 学院毕业后，做了几年中学教师便转行当作家和记者。一战时为《每日邮报》Daily Mail 写战事报道并以此获贵族头衔（KBE），为 《看客》The Spectator 写稿近30年。他还写了大量描绘英国乡村生活的文章，如 Village England (1935), The English Landscape (1938)，the Way of a Countryman (1944)。

背景

A Countryman’s Creed 初版于1946年，早在1931年，Thomas 就一直在痛斥英国的英格兰和威尔士农民联合会（National Farmers Union of England and Wales）不设法阻止农业衰落。1945年7月，英国工党（Labour Party）赢得大选（丘吉尔虽赢了二战却下台），为战后重建，加快发展工业。而一战以来，英国农村巨变，非复往昔，Thomas 主张复兴旧日的田园生活。
