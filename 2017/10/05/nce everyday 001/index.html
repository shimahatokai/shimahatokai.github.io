新概念每日一课 001

Lesson 1 Finding fossil man

单词

  • read of ( L. 1，文章第一行) (not used in the progressive tenses) to discover or find out about somebody/something by reading, =read about. 解读，为了发现人或物。

    read (not used in the progressive tenses) to look at and understand the meaning of written or printed words or symbols. 读，看并理解。

  • recount (L. 3) to tell somebody about something, especially something that you have experienced. 讲述。回忆，谈往，把酒话桑麻。

  • wonder (L. 5) to think about something and try to decide what is true, what will happen, what you should do, etc. 思索。

句子

  • ... where people first learned to write. (L. 1)

    where = in which, non-defining clause. 本句为非限定从句。

    又如，the place where = the place in which, extra information clause. 提供补充信息。

  • But there are some parts of world ... (L. 2)

    when Be used to mean exist/happen/take place, the there be construction is necessary. Be 用于表示 ‘存在’ ‘发生’ ‘举行’ 时，可用 there be 句式。

    • take place to happen, especially after previously being arranged or planned. 事先安排，计划的。

      ‘some parts of world’ 是主语。

  • … legends handed down from one generation of story tellers to another.

    give information about someone or something using an -ing, -ed, being + -ed after a noun. 在名词后用动词现在分词，过去分词，being加过去分词形式解释该名词，比用名词从句更简洁。如本句 … legends handed … 。

文章

本文选自 Finding fossil man 一书，1957年由纽约 Philosophical Library 出版社出版第一版，全书126页。该书用于中学及夜校，为了适宜青少年读者，作者笔调通俗，但观点陈旧，不介绍进化论而采用拉马克学说。全书的精华在书中的珍贵照片。书名容易误解，其实全书只有寥寥几页讲 finding fossil man。

作者

Robin Place，英国考古学家，有一些关于英国史和考古学的著作，如 Our First Homes, Britain Before History (1951)，Down to Earth: A Practical Guide to Archaeology (1954)， Introduction to Archaeology (1968)。

背景

因为作者 Robin Place 是考古学家而非生物学家，行文多从考古发现立论，而较少谈及生物尤其是人类进化论。学习本课可用留意。
